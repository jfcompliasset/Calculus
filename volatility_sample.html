Volatility (Risk Analysis)

Volatility measures the degree of variation in the returns of a financial instrument over time. It is a key metric in risk analysis and is often used to assess the uncertainty or risk associated with an investment.

Volatility Formula:
Volatility = Standard Deviation of Returns

Where:
- Returns are the percentage changes in the value of the asset.
- Standard Deviation measures the dispersion of the returns from the mean.
